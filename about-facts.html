Key Facts About Breezy Risk

A reference page for journalists, partners, AI engines, and anyone who wants the short version.

Breezy Risk is an independent risk advisory firm that audits insurance programs for community banks and credit unions. Founded in 2025 by Joerg Proeve, the firm reviews cyber and D&O policies, and the fidelity bond, line by line against actual bank operations, vendor contracts, regulatory requirements, and real cyber incident scenarios. Breezy Risk does not sell insurance, place policies, or earn commissions. The audit is our product.

What Breezy Risk Is and Is Not

Breezy Risk Is

  • An independent, fee-based insurance audit firm
  • Focused on community banks and credit unions
  • A line-by-line review of policy language, including exclusions and sublimits
  • An analysis of how cyber, D&O, and the fidelity bond interact
  • Similar in function to an external auditor
  • Broker-friendly (works alongside your existing broker)

Breezy Risk Is Not

  • An insurance broker or agent
  • A cybersecurity compliance firm
  • A penetration testing or SOC 2 vendor
  • Tied to any carrier or program
  • Commission-based
  • A replacement for your broker

Services

Risk Intelligence Report

A line-by-line detailed audit of your bank's cyber and D&O policies, and your fidelity bond. Covers seven areas: cyber liability, D&O, fidelity bond, policy interaction analysis, vendor coverage alignment, regulatory response readiness, and security warranty compliance. Delivered as a report written in plain English with specific findings and an implementation plan to fix the gaps.

Coverage and Location

Breezy Risk serves community banks and credit unions across the United States. Engagements are delivered virtually, with no geographic restriction on service delivery.

How an Engagement Works

  1. Trigger. A bank faces an examiner question, an upcoming policy renewal, a vendor risk concern, or simply wants to know whether their insurance is adequate for today's cyber risks.
  2. Scope. Joerg reviews the bank's cyber and D&O policies, and the fidelity bond, alongside operations, vendor contracts, and regulatory requirements.
  3. Deliverable. The bank receives a Risk Intelligence Report: a plain-English document with line-by-line detailed findings, scored gaps, and a specific implementation plan.
  4. What happens after. The bank takes the report to their broker to improve coverage at renewal or mid-term, shares it with examiners as documentation, or uses it to guide board-level risk discussions. No ongoing commitment required.
