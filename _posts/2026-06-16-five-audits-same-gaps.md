Over the past months, I audited the insurance policies at five community banks and credit unions. I read the policy forms, endorsements, and amendments across their cyber and D&O policies, and their fidelity bonds. Asset sizes ranged from under $500 million to over $1 billion. Different carriers. Different program structures.

The gaps were similar. Five structural problems appeared in every program. That pattern changes the conversation. One bank with a gap is a broker discussion. Five banks with five different programs and the same gaps is an industry problem.

Every bank had social engineering coverage on its fidelity bond. Every endorsement had catches: sublimits of $250,000 to $500,000, verification warranties that void coverage if not followed, and a forensic classification swing of up to $4.75 million at one bank. On a $475,000 wire fraud loss, recovery ranged from $0 to $250,000.

Five for five. Every D&O policy excluded claims arising from a cyber event. The cyber carrier says board liability is a D&O claim. The D&O carrier says it arises from a cyber event. Zero banks had full board protection. Zero had a Side A fallback.

Every bank runs on a single core platform. Coverage for vendor outages ranged from $0 (not purchased) to $5 million. One bank's $100,000 sublimit would exhaust in two days. Two of five only covered security breaches, not system failures.

Combined limits ranged from $12 million to $27 million. Realistic recovery on a multi-front incident: $500,000 to $3 million. The gap was 70 to 85 percent. Sublimits share the aggregate. Defense costs erode from inside. Each carrier points at the others.

Banks earn 70 to 85 percent of revenue from net interest income. Most cyber BI definitions exclude it. Two banks had a $500/hour flat BI rate against $1,400 to $2,200 in hourly interest income. One bank recovered $48,000 on a $269,000 loss. One carrier offers the fix. One bank had it.

## The Pattern

These findings compound. A single cyber incident triggers all three policies. The cyber carrier denies under a sublimit. The bond carrier denies under a verification condition. The D&O carrier denies under a cyber exclusion. The BI definition excludes the bank's primary revenue. Each carrier points at the others.

The gaps are structural. They appeared across all carriers and program types. The fix is not more insurance. It is reading the insurance you already have.
